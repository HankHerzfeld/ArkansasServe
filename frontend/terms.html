It has not been reviewed by a lawyer and is not a substitute for one. Arkansas Serve is used by minors and court-involved youth, and the sections marked below involve decisions that only the operator and counsel can make.
Who may use Arkansas Serve

Arkansas Serve is for students, adult volunteers, schools, juvenile detention and court departments, and community organizations tracking community-service hours in Arkansas.

Users under 18 must provide a parent or guardian's details, and a guardian must consent to their participation. The platform determines whether you are a minor from your date of birth — not from the account type you pick — so this cannot be skipped by selecting "adult volunteer".

Your account

Accounts are created and signed in through Microsoft Entra External ID. Keep your sign-in credentials to yourself; you are responsible for activity under your account.

Provide accurate information. Service hours are used by schools and courts to verify that a real obligation was met, so misrepresenting your identity, your hours, or your attendance undermines the record for everyone.

Organizations and roles

You hold a separate membership in each organization you join, and your role is granted per organization. Joining an organization lets its administrators see your profile, sign-ups, and hours with them, so they can verify your service.

A membership you added yourself can be removed by you. A membership an organization created for you — for example a school adding you to its roster — can only be removed by that organization's administrators.

Service hours

Hours are submitted by you or recorded by an organizer, and become part of your record only once an administrator approves them. Administrators may reject or reverse hours that were logged in error or cannot be verified. Approval reflects an organization's judgement, not a guarantee by Arkansas Serve that any school, court, or other body will accept them.

Acceptable use

Do not use Arkansas Serve to harass, endanger, or misrepresent anyone; to access records you have not been given a role to see; or to upload content you do not have the right to share. Because the platform holds information about minors, misuse of another person's records is treated seriously and may end your access.

Privacy

How your information is collected, used, and shared is described in the Privacy Policy, which forms part of these terms.

Still to be completed

These are legal positions for the operator and counsel to set, and are deliberately left blank rather than guessed at.

  • Service availability and warranties — what, if anything, is promised about uptime and accuracy.
  • Limitation of liability — including where a school or court relies on hours recorded here.
  • Suspension and termination — grounds, notice, and what happens to a person's service record afterwards.
  • Governing law and dispute resolution — expected to be Arkansas.
  • Agreements with schools and organizations — how these terms relate to any separate contract, and who is the data controller.
  • Changes to these terms — how notice is given. The platform can re-prompt for acceptance when the version changes; the notice period is a policy decision.
