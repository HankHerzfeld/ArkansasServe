Privacy Policy

Version 2026-07-14-draft · Last updated 14 July 2026

Draft — not yet reviewed by legal counsel. This describes how the system actually behaves today, written from the source code so it is accurate rather than boilerplate. It has not been reviewed by a lawyer and is not a substitute for one. Arkansas Serve handles records for minors and court-involved youth, which brings obligations (FERPA, COPPA, and Arkansas juvenile record law among them) that must be assessed by counsel before this page is relied on.
What this covers

Arkansas Serve is a service-hour tracking platform used by schools, juvenile detention/court departments, and community organizations in Arkansas. This page explains what personal information the platform stores, why, and who can see it.

Information we store

Taken from the fields the application actually persists.

Account & identity. Your name (first, last, and the display name shown to others) and email address. Sign-in itself is handled by Microsoft Entra External ID — Arkansas Serve never receives or stores your password.

Profile. Whether you are a student or an adult volunteer, your date of birth, phone number, and (for students) your grade.

Guardian details, for users under 18. Guardian name, guardian email and/or phone, and a record that guardian consent was given. Date of birth — not what you select for yourself — determines whether these are required.

Adult volunteers. Affiliation and emergency contact name and phone.

Background-check status. Whether a check is recorded as pending or cleared, and the date. This is set by an administrator, never self-reported, and the platform does not store the background check itself.

Service activity. Organizations you belong to and your role in each, events you sign up for, answers to any sign-up questions an organizer asks, hours logged, and whether those hours were approved.

Uploads. Any verification documents or event photos you or an organization upload.

Who can see your information

Arkansas Serve keeps a separate record of you in each organization you belong to. An organization's administrators can see the record you have with them — not your membership in unrelated organizations.

Administrators at a school or organization you belong to can see your profile, your sign-ups, and your service hours in order to verify them. Platform administrators (Arkansas Serve staff) can see records across organizations in order to operate and support the service.

Administrator access to your account

A platform administrator can open a time-limited, audited session that shows them the application as you see it, to diagnose a problem. Every such session records who did it, who they viewed, when, and why, and every change made during one is logged. Sessions expire automatically and can be revoked.

Today this is limited to demonstration accounts only — it cannot be used on a real person's account. If that ever changes, the person whose account is viewed will be notified.

Where your information is held

Data is stored in Microsoft Azure (databases and file storage) in the United States. Sign-in is provided by Microsoft Entra External ID. Notification emails, where enabled, are sent through Azure Communication Services. Traffic is served over HTTPS.
